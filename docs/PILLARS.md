# The 5 Content Pillars

Every campaign calendar should balance five content pillars at roughly 20% each across a month. This balance keeps your audience engaged, prevents fatigue from any single message type, and ensures you are educating, building trust, and converting in equal measure.

---

## Pillar 1: Educational

**What it is:** Content that teaches your audience something useful. These emails position your brand as an expert and build trust before asking for the sale. The goal is to make the reader smarter, more informed, or more confident in their purchase decision.

**When to use it:**
- Early in the week (Tuesday/Wednesday) when engagement is highest
- After a heavy sales push to reset the relationship
- When you have blog content, research, or product science to share
- To warm up new subscribers before hitting them with offers

**Campaign angle examples:**
1. "Myth-Busting: [Category]" -- numbered myth list with check/X format
2. "Top Recovery Tips: Diet Edition" -- tips and checklist format
3. "Three Ingredients. Zero Fillers." -- ingredient transparency
4. "Clogged Pores? No More!" -- problem/solution angle
5. "Your Hair. Your Journey. Your Routine." -- educational routine walkthrough
6. "5 Utensils That Belong in Every Kitchen" -- tips and list
7. "Make Meal Prep a Breeze" -- lifestyle education
8. "[Ingredient] 101: What it Actually Does" -- ingredient deep-dive
9. "Step 1: [Action]. Step 2: [Action]." -- routine or how-to
10. "[Brand Blog] Will Ruin Takeout Forever" -- blog CTA angle
11. "Get Recipe: [Seasonal Recipe]" -- recipe email
12. "What's NOT in Our [Product]" -- transparency / clean-label angle
13. "The Science Behind [Benefit]" -- research-backed content
14. "[Category] FAQ: Your Top 5 Questions Answered" -- FAQ roundup

**Best Figma modules:** Myth-Busting Numbered List, Us vs. Them Comparison, Numbered Tips Module, Step-by-Step Routine, Recipe Block, Blog CTA Block, Ingredient Callout Icons, Tip Callout Block

---

## Pillar 2: Social Proof

**What it is:** Emails that let your customers do the selling for you. Reviews, testimonials, UGC, media features, before/after transformations, and volume-based trust signals. Social proof reduces purchase anxiety and validates the decision to buy.

**When to use it:**
- Mid-week or after an educational email to reinforce credibility
- When launching a new product (show early adopter reviews)
- When a product has strong review volume or star ratings to highlight
- To re-engage subscribers who have browsed but not purchased

**Campaign angle examples:**
1. "Real Stories, Real Heat" -- UGC and testimonials
2. "Real Hair Problems, Solved" -- before/after plus reviews
3. "17,000+ Believers Can't Be Wrong..." -- social proof volume
4. "SPICY TESTIMONIALS" -- fun testimonial roundup
5. "What Our Customers Are Saying" -- review grid
6. "Crystal's Story" -- single customer spotlight
7. "These Hats Have Earned Their Stripes" -- repeat buyer loyalty story
8. "Trusted By Thousands. Verified By Science." -- dual proof angle
9. "See Why [X] People Switched" -- conversion/switching story
10. "Before and After: [Product] Results" -- transformation showcase
11. "Our Most-Reviewed Product Ever" -- review milestone
12. "[X.X] Stars Across [X,000]+ Reviews" -- aggregate rating spotlight

**Best Figma modules:** Single Testimonial Full-Width, Multi-Review Grid, Star Rating Bar, Customer Story Spotlight, Before/After Module, UGC Block, SPICY TESTIMONIALS, Repeat Buyer Badge

## Pillar 3: Community / Branded

**What it is:** Emails that build emotional connection to the brand. Founder stories, behind-the-scenes content, mission and values, cause tie-ins, giveaways, referral programs, and loyalty plays. These emails make people feel like they belong to something, not just buy from something.

**When to use it:**
- Around holidays that align with your brand values (Earth Day, Veterans Day, etc.)
- When you want to humanize the brand after a stretch of product/sales emails
- To reward loyal customers or drive referrals
- When you have a genuine story to tell (founding story, milestone, give-back initiative)

**Campaign angle examples:**
1. "How It All Began..." -- brand origin story
2. "GROW & BEHOLD" -- brand values and mission
3. "Every Pair Tells a Story" -- brand identity
4. "Beauty With A Purpose" -- mission/cause
5. "In Honor of Earth Day" -- cause tie-in with VIP exclusive code
6. "We're Giving Away [Prize]" -- giveaway/contest
7. "Refer a Friend -- Earn $20" -- referral program
8. "JOIN THE GREATS" -- community/loyalty
9. "Corporate Gifts That Say More" -- B2B/gifting angle
10. "A Note From Our Founder" -- personal founder message
11. "Why We Started [Brand]" -- origin and mission
12. "Behind the Scenes: How [Product] Gets Made" -- process story

**Best Figma modules:** Founder Story Block, Brand Mission Block, Giveaway Entry Block, Referral Program Block, Digital Gift Card Block, Cause/Holiday Tie-In Block

## Pillar 4: Product / Collection

**What it is:** Emails that showcase what you sell. Best sellers, new drops, restocks, seasonal collections, gift guides, product spotlights, category navigation, and bundles. These emails drive discovery and move people closer to purchase without a discount.

**When to use it:**
- When you have new arrivals, restocks, or seasonal collections to announce
- Before major gifting holidays (gift guides)
- When you want to drive revenue without discounting
- To highlight best sellers or underperforming SKUs that need visibility

**Campaign angle examples:**
1. "The Best from the Deep Blue" -- product spotlight
2. "Send a Meal That Brightens Their Day" -- gifting angle
3. "A Taste of the Ocean, Straight to Their Door" -- sensory/product
4. "Restocked: [Product Name]" -- restock urgency
5. "New Drop: [Product]" -- launch announcement
6. "New Colors & Soulful Extras" -- collection expansion
7. "The Hat Pros Hall of Fame" -- best sellers spotlight
8. "The Ultimate Gift Guide For Him" -- gift guide
9. "Shop By Category" -- collection navigation
10. "[Product] -- made different." -- differentiation
11. "What's On Sale: [Category]" -- sale preview
12. "A Game-Changing Duo" -- product pairing/bundle
13. "Our Top 5 This Month" -- monthly best sellers
14. "Limited Stock: [Product]" -- scarcity-driven spotlight

**Best Figma modules:** Single Product Spotlight, 2/3/4-Product Grid, Best Sellers Callout, Shop By Category, New Arrivals Strip, Product Feature Breakdown, Bundle/Combo Module, Size Selector Display, Add to Box Module

## Pillar 5: Sales

**What it is:** Direct promotional emails with a discount, offer, or deal. Flash sales, BOGO, sitewide percentage off, tiered discounts, holiday sales, last-chance urgency, and extended sales. These emails exist to convert -- they should be punchy, urgent, and clear.

**When to use it:**
- Around major retail holidays (BFCM, Memorial Day, 4th of July, etc.)
- When you have a genuine offer to push (not manufactured urgency)
- To close out inventory or drive a revenue spike
- Paired with countdown timers and urgency language for maximum impact

**Campaign angle examples:**
1. "4th of July BBQ Blowout is Live" -- holiday flash sale
2. "TREAT DAD WITH BOGO 50% OFF" -- Father's Day
3. "LAST CHANCE FOR VETS" -- Veterans Day
4. "35% Off Scrubs -- The More You Buy, The More You Save" -- tiered discount
5. "BOGO 50% OFF All Sale Items" -- BOGO
6. "24 Hours Only. Up to 40% Off Sitewide." -- flash sale
7. "Last Chance to Secure These Looks" -- urgency/scarcity
8. "Sale Extended: 25-50% Off Until [Date]" -- extended sale
9. "NEW WEEK | NEW SAVINGS | NEW LOOK" -- weekly cadence sale
10. "Doorbuster Deals: 50% Off [Specific Products]" -- limited product sale
11. "Email Exclusive: 30% Off / Everything Else: 20% Off" -- email-only offer
12. "ORDER NOW & SAVE $15" -- AOV booster
13. "LAST CHANCE: [X]% Off Ends Tonight" -- final push

**Best Figma modules:** Holiday Sale Banner, Countdown Timer Hero, Discount Code Hero, BOGO Hero, Urgency Banner, Tiered Discount Block, BOGO Module, Doorbuster Deals Grid, Email Exclusive Split, Flash Sale Strip, Offer Expiry Block, Price Strike-Through Block, Limited Stock Badge

## Balancing Pillars Across a Month

### Standard month (3x/week = ~13 emails)

| Pillar | Target Count | Percentage |
|---|---|---|
| Educational | 2-3 | ~20% |
| Social Proof | 2-3 | ~20% |
| Community / Branded | 2-3 | ~20% |
| Product / Collection | 2-3 | ~20% |
| Sales | 2-3 | ~20% |

### Weekly rhythm example (3x/week)

- **Email 1 (Tue):** Educational or Social Proof -- build trust early in the week
- **Email 2 (Thu):** Product/Collection or Community -- maintain engagement
- **Email 3 (Sat):** Sales or Product -- capitalize on weekend shopping

### Rules for rotation

- Never send two Sales pillar emails back-to-back
- Never send three of the same pillar in a single week
- Alternate between "give" emails (Educational, Community, Social Proof) and "ask" emails (Product, Sales)
- Each week should touch at least 2-3 different pillars

## Adjustments for Special Months

### BFCM (November)

Sales pillar increases to 30-40% of the calendar. Educational and Community decrease to 10-15% each. The final two weeks of November may be almost entirely Sales and Product emails, with educational/community content front-loaded into the first two weeks.

Typical BFCM week breakdown:
- Week 1-2: Normal balance, build anticipation
- Week 3 (Thanksgiving week): 60%+ Sales/Product
- Week 4 (BFCM/Cyber Monday): 80%+ Sales with urgency, countdown timers, and last-chance messaging

### Product Launch Months

Product/Collection pillar increases to 30-35%. Build a launch sequence:
1. Teaser (Community/Branded) -- "Something big is coming"
2. Announcement (Product) -- "It's here"
3. Social proof (Social Proof) -- early reviews or waitlist testimonials
4. Education (Educational) -- how to use it, ingredients, science
5. Last chance (Sales) -- launch discount expiring

### December (Holiday/Gifting)

Product/Collection and Sales dominate. Gift guides, shipping deadlines, and last-chance messaging take priority. Always include at least one shipping deadline email with the Shipping Guide/Deadline Block module.

### Slow Months (January, August)

Lean into Educational and Community pillars. Use this time to build goodwill, tell brand stories, and educate. Sales can focus on Subscribe & Save or loyalty rewards rather than heavy discounting.
